ADDS VT220

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub with handmade sub legends that are carved into the caps and then filled in. This layout is a copy of the DEC layout for many of their terminals used this layout and have the super thick arrow keys as well.

Connector: PS2 Connector. Switches: NMB Linear, Two Eye, White Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

Memorex Telex

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and this keyboard has a different caps lock key than the rest of the NMB RT101+. Also this keyboard has NKRO and a different single foot instead of feet.

Connector: 5 Pin Din. Switches: NMB Clicky, Two Eye, White Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

Epson Q103A

This keyboard uses a very rare Alps Linear that is normally just found in spacebars of other keyboards that Alps maunfactured. Keycaps are Alps doubleshot ABS.

Connector: 5 Pin Din. Switches: Alps SKCL Cream Protocol: AT Status: Sold

NMB AT

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and this keyboard is part of the second genertion of NMB keyboards.

Connector: 5 Pin Din. Switches: NMB Linear, One Eye, White Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

IBM Model M

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub. This keyboard is one of the most common vintage keyboards to find within North America.

Connector: PS2 Connector. Switches: Buckling Spring Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

OEMMAX MCK-860

This keyboard has had the alpha keys replaced with PBT Dye Sub keycaps becuase the alphas that came on the keyboard originally were not complete.

Connector: 5 Pin Din. Switches: Alps SKCM Blue Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

RT101 Intel

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub. This 4th gen NMB Keyboard is NOS and the tactile switches are nice but not most people's favourite NMB switch.

Connector: PS2 Connector. Switches: NMB Tactile, Two Eye, Grey Protocol: AT Status: Sold

NCD N-97SYS

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and the font that is used on this keyboard is only fond on NCD boards that were manufactured by NMB. This board uses clicky switches that don't have the click arm in them and are molded in white plastic.

Connector: PS2 Connector. Switches: NMB Linear-Clicky, Two Eye, White Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

ICL DRS

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and have sublegends on both the front face of the keycaps and on top as well. This keyboard normally comes with Tacile, Two Eye, Grey but due to poor condition they have been mostly swapped to Linear, Two Eye, Yellow.

Connector: PS2 Connector Switches: NMB Linear, Two Eye, Yellow Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

SKB-5150C

The keycaps are Tai Hao doubleshot ABS. This keyboard uses a early cherry clone and they are linear like MX Blacks. They are not great and the molds that were used to produce the switches are pretty bad.

Connector: 5 Pin Din. Switches: KeyWorld Protocol: AT Status: Sold

WYSE Gate Array

This keyboard has doubleshot ABS keycaps and the MX Vint Blacks are NOS.

Connector: 4P4C Switches: Cherry MX Black Protocol: WYSE Status: In Collection

RT101+

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and this keyboard is the 4th generation of the NMB keyboards.

SMK-85E

This keyboard was manufactured by SMK and this keyboard uses Alps SKBM switches that now have been linearized. They were linearized because the switches were not consistent across the whole board.

Connector: PS2 Connector Switches: Alps SKBM Black Protocol: AT Status: In Collection

NMB PC122 Rev.C

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub. This keyboard is the second known version of the PC122 and it is the cheaper version of the battleship. This keyboard is technically a clone of the IBM F122.

Connector: 5 Pin Din. Switches: NMB Clicky, Two Eye, Black Protocol: AT/XT Status: In Collection

Duck Raven

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub from a SGI 101. This keyboard has NOS Alps SKCM Browns.

Connector: USB Mini B Switches: Alps SKCM Brown Protocol: USB Status: In Collection

ADDS ASCII

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and this keybaord includes a lot of blank caps because the use of the function keys could vary so there is a ribbon that tells the user what the function keys do. This keyboard also can be switched between either the ADDS protocol or the AT protocol based on the jumpers that are soldered on the PCB.

Connector: 4P4C. Switches: NMB Linear, Two Eye, White Protocol: AT/ADDS Status: In Collection

Tandy1200HD

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and don't have clips on them because they are 1st gen keycaps. The switches that came with this keyboard are prototype switches. This keyboard was damaged when I got it therefore the rest of the switches are Charcoal Gundams.

Connector: 5 Pin Din. Switches: NMB Linear, One Eye, Grey/Charcoal Gundams Protocol: XT Status: In Collection

RT101

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and the lock keys for this keyboard use a LED switch instead of a normal switches.

NCD N-108LK

The keycaps are PBT Dye Sub and the lock keys for this keyboard.
